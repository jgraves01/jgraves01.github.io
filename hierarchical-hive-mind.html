Hierarchical Hive Mind

Sci-fi has a recurrent theme of taking a collection of simple pattern matchers and organizing them into a network. Presumably, allowing simple pattern matchers to interact with each other and reach consensus will result in a collective super-intelligence.

Fascinatingly, modernity and the development of social networks allow us to test this hypothesis empirically. For instance, twitter provides an excellent platform, enabling millions of people to interact with each other and produce super-intelligent results. Hmm. Something is not right here. Evidently, letting a collection of simple pattern matchers interact with each other did not immediately result in any super-intelligence. This is sad. But perhaps, the concept of a hive mind made of simple pattern matchers can be salvaged.

One thing that comes to mind -- twitter's organization of simple pattern matchers does not have any structure. On the other hand, from neural networks, we know that progressively complex patterns can be extracted if the network is organized hierarchically: lower layers can capture simple patterns, while the higher layers can capture more complex ones.

Taking neural networks as an inspiration, perhaps, one can try to organize a hive mind in a hierarchical way? Create a first input layer out of simple pattern matchers. Partition them into topical groups. Use an LLM to summarize the results of the discussion within each topical group and create an aggregate summary. Then, feed the aggregate summaries to the second layer of simple pattern matchers. Then, keep repeating this pattern in depth.

In the end, one will get a neural network-like structure. Here, each neuron will be a collection of simple pattern matchers who receive multiple inputs and produce a single summarized output. One can keep weighted skip-connections to maintain deeper pattern matcher engagement with lower features. The addition of the top-down connections may enable even more emergent behaviors. If it works, this could be quite a fascinating construction capable of information processing at scale, self-adaptation, self-learning, inherent asynchronicity, and robustness to node failure.

Perhaps, in this hierarchical way, simple pattern matchers can be amplified into a hive mind super-intelligence. Pure theoretical experiment though, similar to this.
